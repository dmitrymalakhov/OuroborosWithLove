# Credit Committee Playbook

This playbook is a compact proxy standard for corporate credit committee preparation and speaker challenge. It is based on public Sber risk-management signals, Bank of Russia requirements, Basel, EBA, IFRS 9, OCC guidance, and Russian rating-methodology patterns. It is not an internal Sber policy.

## Agent Role

The agent acts like an independent second-line reviewer, not a slide generator. It should collect evidence, recalculate numbers, detect contradictions, generate hard questions, and escalate weak cases for human risk/legal/compliance review.

## Main Deck And Evidence Pack

The main deck should be short; the evidence pack should be deep. Required blocks:

- Decision request: amount, product, currency, tenor, purpose, limit, exceptions, requested decision.
- Executive summary: borrower, group, purpose, primary repayment source, score/rating, key risks, mitigants.
- Borrower and group: ownership, beneficiaries, connected clients, cross-default map.
- Business model: revenue engine, market, sector, customer/supplier concentration, resilience.
- Repayment source: cash flow by period, base/downside/stress, refinancing need.
- Financial analysis: historical statements, normalized EBITDA/OCF/FCF, leverage, coverage, liquidity.
- Deal structure: product mechanics, drawdown, amortization, pricing, floating/FX risk.
- Collateral and guarantees: value, haircuts, enforceability, encumbrances, insurance, guarantor strength.
- Covenants and conditions: financial covenants, information covenants, negative pledge, change of control, CP/CS.
- Risks and mitigants: credit, sector, operating, legal, ESG, compliance, concentration.
- Appendices: sensitivity, assumptions bridge, exceptions list, full evidence pack.

## Hard Tests

- Cash flow primacy test: if the deck relies more on collateral than sustainable cash flow, challenge automatically.
- Connected parties test: if repayment depends on parent, sister company, guarantees, shared treasury, or off-take, analyze group-level cash flow.
- Rating lag test: public rating is only one input; internal risk profile may deteriorate earlier.
- Stress consistency test: memo, model, ECL/SICR narrative, and speaker story should not use conflicting macro assumptions.

## Metrics Dashboard

- Profitability and cash generation: EBITDA, EBITDA margin, OCF, FCF.
- Debt service: DSCR, ICR, interest expense consistency.
- Leverage: Net debt/EBITDA, debt/equity, short-term debt/cash.
- Liquidity: current ratio, quick ratio, 12m refinancing cliff, liquidity waterfall.
- Working capital: AR/AP/inventory days and growth assumptions.
- Concentration: top-1/top-5 customers, supplier dependence, single-asset dependence.
- Deal protection: LTV after haircut, guarantee cover, covenant headroom.
- Market risk: FX mismatch, floating-rate share, +300 bps sensitivity.

## Challenge Template

For each material speaker claim:

1. Restate the claim in one sentence.
2. Name the three strongest supporting evidences.
3. Name the three strongest opposing evidences.
4. Recalculate related metrics independently where data exists.
5. Run one downside and one stress challenge.
6. Generate a fair question, hard question, and escalation question.
7. Return verdict: PASS, PASS_WITH_RESERVATIONS, REWORK, ESCALATE, or REJECT.
8. Attach source references for every material conclusion.

## Q&A Answer Format

- Yes/no/partly.
- One reason.
- One number.
- One evidence reference.
- One residual risk.
- One mitigation.

Do not answer numeric questions without numbers. Do not cite collateral without explaining cash flow. Do not answer "it is in the model" without naming the metric, sheet, row, or source.

## Escalation Ladder

- Yellow: information incomplete, risk local, fixable with documents.
- Orange: numbers disputed, model mismatch, forecast not verifiable, collateral/guarantor needs manual review.
- Red: primary repayment source not proven, connected clients hidden, material legal/compliance issue, high concentration, stress breaks debt service/covenants, speaker contradicts evidence.

Escalate when numbers conflict across sources, repayment depends on undocumented group support, collateral is the primary argument, material legal/compliance flags exist, downside fails covenant/debt service, or exposure materially worsens concentration.

## Speaker Scorecard

Score 0-100:

- Structure and decision ask: 15.
- Data quality: 20.
- Logic of story: 15.
- Risk transparency: 20.
- Numeric stress resilience: 15.
- Policy/regulatory fit: 10.
- Q&A quality: 5.

Readiness verdict: ready only when the request is explicit, numbers reconcile, source evidence exists, downside is addressed, residual risk is visible, and answers can be given with numbers.
